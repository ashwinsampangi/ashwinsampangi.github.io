Finance, run on AI

Most finance leaders experiment with AI. I rebuilt how a finance function runs on it.

With the verification, review, and governance a CFO would demand. The differentiator is not that I use AI — it is that I made its output trustworthy enough to put in front of a board.

01 — The framework

There are five levels of using AI

Most people never leave the first one — and most strong operators stop at the third. The jump that compounds is from automating tasks to encoding judgment.

1
Ask

Questions to a chatbot when you are stuck. Useful, occasional, nothing compounds.

2
Draft

AI writes the first version, you clean it up. The ceiling is still you, from scratch.

3
Template

A library of saved prompts for repeat tasks. Where most strong operators stop.

4
Automate

Custom tools that do real work end to end, without you in the loop each time.

5
Compound The rare one

Judgment encoded as rules; every correction captured so it never recurs; system and operator sharpen together each cycle. The AI executes — the judgment and accountability stay with me.

02 — How it works

The engine is not the model. It is the loop.

Three of the four steps are mine. The AI executes; the judgment and the accountability do not move.

03 — In practice

One recurring task, run through the loop

A representative example — sanitized, no company figures. The recurring task is the quarterly board update package; the walk-through zooms into one component — the variance commentary — to show the four steps end to end.

The task
Quarterly board update package
Recurring · quarterly board cycle
Zoomed in: the variance commentary
Define · mine
Set the bar before anything is written

Every variance over threshold gets a driver, an owner, and a next action — and volume effects are separated from rate effects. Nothing ships that a board member could misread as cause when it is only correlation.

Draft · system
Pull, write, and cite at speed

It reconciles actuals against plan, writes commentary for each line, and tags every figure with its source and as-of date. Anything it cannot source, it flags rather than guesses.

Refine · mine
Correct with judgment, not a red pen

Two corrections this cycle: it had named a correlation as the driver on one line, and double-counted two effects on another. I fix the reasoning, not just the wording.

Encode · mine
Make the fix permanent

Both corrections become standing rules — always split volume vs. rate; never attribute a driver without a supporting cut. Dated, reversible, and applied automatically next quarter.

The outcome

What used to take two analysts most of a week is now a same-day first draft — and it arrives already carrying its sources, so review is spent checking judgment, not chasing numbers. Next quarter, this cycle's two corrections are already baked in.

04 — The proof

Built, governed, and measured

One task is an anecdote. The point is that the same discipline runs across the function — at scale, with the controls in place.

48
Custom workflows

Board prep, scenario analysis, variance commentary, investor Q&A, and capital allocation — on a structured business-memory layer.

4 gates
Verification protocol

Source, recency, consistency, citation — every number and claim checked before it ships, then adversarial review and a self-grade on top.

3× / 2×
Output, personal / team

Multi-week analyses now take days; dashboards that took months take days; full adoption across the team.

Every number it produces carries its source and its as-of date, or it does not ship — never a guess a board could act on. Anything headed for a board self-grades against a seven-point bar and gets rewritten until it clears. That is the part most AI skips, and the only part a CFO actually cares about.
