03 — In practice

One recurring task, run through the loop

A representative example — sanitized, no company figures. This is what the four steps look like on a task that repeats every quarter.

The task
Quarterly board variance commentary
Recurring · quarterly close
Actuals vs. plan, line by line
Define · mine
Set the bar before anything is written

Every variance over threshold gets a driver, an owner, and a next action — and volume effects are separated from rate effects. Nothing ships that a board member could misread as cause when it's only correlation.

Draft · system
Pull, write, and cite at speed

It reconciles actuals against plan, writes commentary for each line, and tags every figure with its source and as-of date. Anything it can't source, it flags rather than guesses.

Refine · mine
Correct with judgment, not a red pen

Two corrections this cycle: it had named a correlation as the driver on one line, and double-counted two effects on another. I fix the reasoning, not just the wording.

Encode · mine
Make the fix permanent

Both corrections become standing rules — always split volume vs. rate; never attribute a driver without a supporting cut. Dated, reversible, and applied automatically next quarter.

The outcome

What used to take two analysts most of a week is now a same-day first draft — and it arrives already carrying its sources, so review is spent checking judgment, not chasing numbers. Next quarter, this cycle's two corrections are already baked in.
